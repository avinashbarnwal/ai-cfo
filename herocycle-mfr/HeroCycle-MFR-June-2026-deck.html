Revenue & driver quality

June revenue of INR 142.6 Cr beat budget by 5.6% and grew 17.5% over prior year, driven by volume rather than price: units beat plan by 5.7% while AOV of INR 12,043 was flat to budget. The quality of the beat is mixed by design — e-cycles (+34% YoY, INR 7.4 Cr above budget) and D2C (+18% vs budget) did the work, while the standard segment declined 4% YoY and the dealer network was marginally below plan. The CFO's read, adopted for this review: growth is durable but its composition is deliberately shifting toward newer channels and categories, and planning assumptions on mix should be updated rather than treating June as an anomaly.

Margin & profit conversion

A +INR 7.6 Cr revenue beat converted into a -1.2 Cr EBITDA miss. The bridge explains it without ambiguity: volume added +2.1 Cr, but channel mix took -1.6 Cr, input costs and discounting -0.8 Cr, and marketing phasing -1.2 Cr. Gross margin of 27.8% is 170 bps below budget and the first meaningful break in an otherwise stable 29%+ trend across the prior five months. The chosen interpretation: this is a structural mix effect that will persist as long as marketplace and D2C outgrow dealers — it requires a pricing and channel-economics response, not a cost-cutting one. Marketing is treated as timing, to be verified against July actuals.

Cash & working capital

Cash closed at INR 34.2 Cr, down 6.8 Cr from May's 41.0 Cr. EBITDA contributed +11.2 Cr, offset by the pre-festive inventory build (-5.4 Cr), dealer receivables (-3.1 Cr), the e-cycle capacity investment (-8.6 Cr), and tax and other outflows (-2.6 Cr), partly cushioned by payables (+1.7 Cr). The review's judgment: this is predominantly planned investment ahead of the festive season rather than deterioration, but two items carry follow-up obligations — inventory must sell through in Q2, and the AR extension to dealers needs an explicit credit policy rather than ad-hoc accommodation. If both persist into the August close, the cash conversation changes character.
